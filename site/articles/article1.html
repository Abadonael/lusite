Management of Thyroid Nodules: A Practical Guide

April 20, 2026

Evidence-based approach to evaluation and management of thyroid nodules, from initial detection to treatment.

Thyroid nodules are extremely common, found in up to 65% of the general population on ultrasound examination. The challenge lies in identifying the small percentage that represent malignancy.

Initial Evaluation

All patients with thyroid nodules should undergo TSH testing and ultrasound examination. Ultrasound characteristics help stratify malignancy risk using systems such as ACR TI-RADS or ATA guidelines.

Fine Needle Aspiration

FNA biopsy is indicated for nodules meeting size and sonographic criteria. Cytology results are reported using the Bethesda classification system, guiding subsequent management.

Molecular Testing

For indeterminate cytology, molecular testing can help refine malignancy risk. Tests evaluating gene mutations and expression patterns have improved diagnostic accuracy.
